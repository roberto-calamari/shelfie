# Brand Starter Package — Shelfie

## Recommended Name

### **Shelfie**

A portmanteau of "shelf" + "selfie" — instantly communicates both the bookish context and the social sharing intent. Short, memorable, playful without being juvenile. Works as a verb ("I just made a Shelfie") and a noun ("Check out my Shelfie").

## Backup Names

1. **Spineshot** — "spine" (book spine) + "shot" (photo). More punchy/graphic.
2. **Pagecard** — Straightforward. "Page" + "card". Clear but less personality.
3. **Bookframe** — Simple. Frames the book for social sharing.
4. **Afterword** — The section after a book ends. Elegant, literary, slightly more serious.
5. **Bindery** — Where books are bound. Craft-forward. Could feel too niche.

## Brand Direction

### Positioning
A focused, beautiful sharing tool — not a book catalog, not a design tool. The emotional promise is: *"Finally, this solves the exact annoying thing I hate."*

### Tone
- Warm but not cutesy
- Bookish but not old-fashioned
- Social-native but not trend-chasing
- Confident but not loud
- A little literary, a little editorial

### Voice Examples
- ✅ "Beautiful story cards for the books you just finished."
- ✅ "Find a Book"
- ✅ "Your story is ready."
- ✅ "This cover isn't as crisp as we'd like. You can still use it or swap in another."
- ❌ "Create your reading journey!" (too corporate)
- ❌ "Share your literary adventures!" (too cheesy)
- ❌ "Powered by AI" (irrelevant to the user)

### Visual Identity

**Logo direction:** A lowercase wordmark in a refined serif (Lora or similar), with a subtle nod to book form. Options:
- Simple lowercase serif wordmark: `shelfie`
- Wordmark with a tiny book spine icon integrated into the `l` or `f`
- The `s` could subtly evoke a book page curl

**Colors:**
- Shell: `#FAF7F2` (warm cream)
- Accent: `#C4956A` (warm amber/copper)
- Text: `#3D3530` (warm dark)
- Surface: `#F5F0E8`

The palette reads as: warm paper, copper foil, reading-nook comfort.

**Typography:**
- UI: DM Sans (clean, modern, friendly)
- Editorial accents: Lora (serif, literary weight without feeling heavy)
- Story cards: Varies by style — Lora for Dreamy/Retro, sans for Cinematic

### Iconography
If an app icon is needed:
- A simple book shape with a small camera/share indicator
- Or the `S` from the wordmark in a rounded square
- Warm amber accent on cream background
- Clean, iOS-native feel

### Social
- Instagram handle: @shelfie.app or @useshelfie
- Hashtag: #shelfie or #myshelfie
- Bio: "Beautiful story cards for books. Free & open source."

## Future Brand Notes

- The brand should never feel more important than the user's book. The book is always the hero.
- Branding in the app is intentionally minimal — a subtle footer link, an optional watermark. Creator-first.
- If the project grows, the brand can evolve toward a more premium editorial direction without needing a full rebrand.
